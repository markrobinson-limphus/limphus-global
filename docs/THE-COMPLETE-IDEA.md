# Limphus Global — The Complete Picture

**One line:**  
You sell *fine mitigation*, not IT. In 2026, firms don’t buy “security.” They buy *not getting fined*. Limphus is the platform that shows them exactly how much they’re exposed, and gives MSPs the one-click advisory to close the deal.

---

## Part 1: The Shift (Why 2026 Is the Year)

### The Old Game (Dead)

- MSPs sold “IT support” and “we’ll keep you secure.”
- Buyers heard “another vendor” and pushed back on price.
- Compliance was a checkbox: “We’re compliant.” No number, no urgency.

### The New Game (2026)

- **Regulators stopped asking and started enforcing.**  
  NYDFS, NIS2, HIPAA 2026, CCPA, SRA — they’re not “guidance” anymore. They’re *mandatory* and they have **fixed penalty numbers**.
- **Fines are public and huge.**  
  $2M+ (NYDFS), €10M or 2% of turnover (NIS2), $2.1M+ annual cap (HIPAA Tier 4). One missing control = one fine. No “we didn’t know.”
- **Insurance and licences depend on it.**  
  LawPRO, malpractice carriers, and regulators all point at the same thing: *baseline controls*. DMARC, MFA, encryption. If you don’t have them, you’re not “compliant” — you’re **exposed**.
- **So the market isn’t buying “IT services.”**  
  It’s buying **fine mitigation**. “Fix this before the 2026 audit” is the pitch. The number on the screen is the hook.

**Bottom line:** The gap between “what the law now requires” and “what most firms have” is a **$248B+** problem. You’re not selling a tool. You’re selling the bridge over that gap.

## Part 2: What Limphus Actually Is

**Limphus Global** is a **multi-tenant SaaS** that turns “we should do something about security” into **proof + one-click advisory**.

- **For you (the MSP / sovereign):**  
  A **Command Center**. You see a **global risk heat map**, a **leads table with fine exposure in dollars**, and **drafts** that are already written with the right regulatory hook (LawPRO, NY RPC 1.6, SRA, HIPAA, NIS2). You click **Approve & Open Gmail** — the email opens, you hit Send. You’re the human in the loop; the machine did the research and the copy.

- **For your clients (the firms):**  
  They get a **clear, authority-backed advisory**: “Under [NYDFS / NIS2 / LawPRO / HIPAA], this gap is a $X exposure. Here’s what to do.” No fluff. No generic “you should secure email.” **Their** jurisdiction, **their** fine number, **your** white-label.

So:  
**Discovery (who’s exposed) → Evidence (audit + fine number) → Draft (regulatory hook) → You approve → One click to Gmail.**  
That’s the whole loop.

## Part 3: The Product (What They See and Use)

### Command Center (Dashboard)

- **Global risk heat map**  
  World map. Countries with leads **glow by fine exposure** (red = higher). NYC and London light up when you have US/UK leads. It’s the “infection map” of non-compliance. No one forgets it.

- **Fine exposure column**  
  Every lead has a number: **$2.00M**, **$10.00M**, etc. That’s the 2026-style exposure (NYDFS, NIS2, HIPAA) based on country + industry. Missing DMARC multiplies it (identity exposure). So the number is **terrifying and accurate**.

- **Bulk lead intake**  
  Paste 100+ domains or URLs. They’re queued, tagged by country/industry, and (when the audit runs) get an exposure number. So you’re not “guessing” who’s hot — the system **ranks them by fine exposure**.

- **Drafts**  
  Each draft is **pre-filled** with:
  - The right **regulatory hook** (e.g. NYDFS Section 500.12, NIS2 Article 21, LawPRO wire fraud).
  - Subject and body that sound like a **compliance officer**, not a vendor.
  - **Approve & Open Gmail** — one click, mailto opens with recipient, subject, body. You send. Next.

- **Live feed / “team above a team”**  
  Hunters (scrapers), Managers (triage, compliance logic), Sovereign (you). It frames the product as **your** command layer over an engine that does the grunt work. You’re not “using software”; you’re **running a compliance operation**.

### Under the Hood (What You Can Say in Sales)

- **Multi-tenant:** Every MSP has their own workspace. Their leads, their drafts, their branding. No one sees anyone else’s data.
- **Regulatory brain:** A single map (e.g. `regulatory_intel.json`) drives hooks and exposure by **region + industry**. US-NY legal ≠ US-NY finance ≠ UK ≠ healthcare. The draft and the number **match the jurisdiction**.
- **Human-on-the-loop:** No automated cold spam. **You** approve. That keeps it consultative, CASL/GDPR-friendly, and high-trust.
- **White-label:** Their logo, their firm name on packs and (when you add PDF export) on the advisory. So it’s **their** report, **your** engine.
